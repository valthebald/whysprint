A mentor is...
In the past two years I've become a mentor, learning from other experienced mentors. I've also organized a bunch of sprints, ranging in size from 5 people to 600 people. I here at dev days because I enjoy working in person with people I normally only work with in the issue queues and irc, to meet new people and be moved by their own drupal stories, and to learn. Talking with people attending the sprints and the session days so far, some of you are here to learn about drupal 8, some to sprint, some will benefit when they go home with skills and knowledge learned here. Some will go home and stir up their local communities with meetups, camps and community they grow. You might go home and implement one small idea, and might help your local community grow.

So, what *is* sprint?

Why people come?

  • Rewarding
  • Education
  • Communication
What makes people contribute back?
It's rewarding - there are multiple ratings based on contribution to Drupal core and contrib.
Drupal.org user profile is a new CV
Education - the best way to know how "things" work is to build them
Remote work is a big thing in the Drupal community, so gathering together is one of the rare opportunities for social communication in real life (Facebook does not count).

Why people come?

We as a community are mostly introverts
You don't have to see someone else's eyes if you don't want to
Actually, you can expect someone playing guitar at Drupal events (and reading books)

Can I do anything?

Stats on Oct 18, 2014

  • 514 Novice issues
  • 3795 patch queue
  • 4781 pending bugs
If your main question "can I do anything", then the answer is definitely yes

Types of tasks

  • Write patches
  • Review patches
  • Issue triage
  • Issue summary update
  • Documentation
  • Localization
There are issues for different groups of Drupal users

But how do I find one for myself?

Go to the issue queue (link in the end of session if you don't know how to find it)
Find issues tagged Novice (or Config Novice, or Twig novice), then sort by last changed in reverse order
