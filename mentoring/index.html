So, what *is* sprint?

Why people come?

  • Rewarding
  • Education
  • Communication
What makes people contribute back?
It's rewarding - there are multiple ratings based on contribution to Drupal core and contrib.
Drupal.org user profile is a new CV
Education - the best way to know how "things" work is to build them
Remote work is a big thing in the Drupal community, so gathering together is one of the rare opportunities for social communication in real life (Facebook does not count).

Why people come?

We as a community are mostly introverts
Well, some of us are extraverts, and you can easily distinguish extravert developer: when talking to you, he/she looks at *your* boots
You don't have to see someone else's eyes if you don't want to
Actually, you can expect someone playing guitar at Drupal events (and reading books)

It's about relevance on the market

Isolated company

  • Source control: any (CVS/SVN/git/SourceSafe/Mercurial)
  • Work environment: any
  • Database: any
  • Communication: any (lynq anyone?)

What tools are in demand?

  • Communication: irc
  • Source control: git
  • drush, xhprof
Last, but not least reason to contribute back to the project - relevance. In the world bound by the Drupal development process, you find companies of different size using the same tool set.
It's git, it's XDebug, sometimes XHProf, drush... whatever
I could be effective on the job market only when I started to contribute, that enforced me to learn tools that are actually required on the job market.
This picture is correct for all Drupal versions up to 7.
It doesn't matter, where are you located in this "diagram"
What matters is that your position is stable
It takes enormous amount of energy to climb the hill
You can comfortable live not only on top, but on all levels of the slope
This is how people on the top part of the slide feel themselves They have enough projects, companies are hunting their heads. What's bad about being on island?
Limited resources: Quickly lose link with other civilizations. Drupal 7 contains some OOP code, but still mostly procedural. And that's when we have new kids on the PHP block: Laravel, Yii, Symfony, Zend Framework
Drupal 8 is different
No more "Drupal island"
Drupal developers - you are out of comfort zone
Nice window of opportunity for newcomers

Is Drupal 8 relevant

Number of tracked Drupal 8 installs is negligible compared to 7.x and even 6.x This week D8 finally went over D5 Check conference agenda - D8 dominates.

Can I do anything?

Stats on Nov 1, 2014

  • 513 Novice issues
  • 3790 patch queue
  • 4813 pending bugs
If your main question "can I do anything", then the answer is definitely yes

Types of tasks

  • Write patches
  • Review patches
  • Issue triage
  • Issue summary update
  • Documentation
  • Localization
There are issues for different groups of Drupal users Basically, we can multiple number of issues 6 fold.

But how do I find one for myself?

Go to the issue queue (link in the end of session if you don't know how to find it)
Find issues tagged Novice (or Config Novice, or Twig novice), then sort by last changed in reverse order
Needs reroll is another good choice for starting
Reroll is a process of adjusting patch that was written once, to the changed source code. If you are interested in specific subsystem of Drupal 8, you can limit issues to that subsystem. Most people don't have preferences, and that's totally fine.
